A Biography of Bjarne Stroustrup

Overview | Career | Awards | Contact Webmaster

Overview

Bjarne Stroustrup, born on December 30th, 1950, was a computer scientist from Denmark who created a programming language known for it's efficiency and versatility and is still used to this day. He developed the C++ programming language in the early 1980s while working at Bell Labs.

Career

He earned a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Cambridge in 1979. Bjarne held academic positions, including at Texas A&M University, and worked in the industry, including positions at Morgan Stanley.

  1. Master's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science from Aarhus University
  2. Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Cambridge
  3. Morgan Stanley

Awards and Recognition

Bjarne Stroustrup's work on C++ revolutionized software development, allowing for greater control and efficiency, which lead to him getting recognized for his work in this field.

  • Received the Dr. Dobb's Excellence in Programming Award.
  • Honored as a fellow of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM).
  • Recognized with the prestigious Charles Stark Draper Prize.
  • Acknowledged as a fellow of the Computer History Museum.
